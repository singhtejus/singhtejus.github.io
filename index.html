About Me

Hi I'm Tejus. I am currently studying Computer Science at UC San Diego, specializing in artificial intelligence, and have pursued an interdisciplinary course of study with a strong emphasis on physics, computer science, and electrical engineering. I am passionate about research-driven and open-ended problems in LLM Reasoning, Machine Learning, and Computational Modeling.

My research lies at the intersection of physics and deep learning architectures. I will soon begin my third year as a research intern at Evovest, where I will develop diffusion models for time series generation. Some of my other projects include Bayesian and stochastic physics-informed modeling.

At UCSD, I previously worked on a hardware implementation of a quantized transformer on fpga. The model classified short-lived particle streams generated by the Large Hadron Collider, so accuracy and fast inference was a focus. During my undergraduate studies at UCI, I primarily researched physics-informed deep learning methods for volumentric medical imaging and had work accepted by the International Journal of Cardiovascular Imaging.
